# IRS Publication 17 - Standard Deduction (2025)

## Standard Deduction Amounts for 2025

The standard deduction is a dollar amount that reduces your taxable income. For tax year 2025, the standard deduction amounts are:

- **Single or Married Filing Separately**: $15,000
- **Married Filing Jointly or Qualifying Surviving Spouse**: $30,000
- **Head of Household**: $22,500

## Additional Standard Deduction

If you are age 65 or older OR blind, you may be entitled to an additional standard deduction:

- **Single or Head of Household**: Additional $1,950
- **Married Filing Jointly or Separately**: Additional $1,550 per qualifying person

## Who Cannot Take the Standard Deduction

You cannot take the standard deduction if:
- You are married filing separately and your spouse itemizes deductions
- You are filing a tax return for a short tax year
- You were a nonresident alien or dual-status alien during the year

## Itemized Deductions vs Standard Deduction

You should itemize deductions if your total itemized deductions are more than your standard deduction. Common itemized deductions include:
- Medical and dental expenses (exceeding 7.5% of AGI)
- State and local taxes (limited to $10,000)
- Home mortgage interest
- Charitable contributions

Reference: Internal Revenue Code §63(c)(2), as adjusted for inflation under §63(c)(4).
