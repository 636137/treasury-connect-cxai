# Treasury Offset Program (TOP) - Offset Process

## What is the Treasury Offset Program?

The Treasury Offset Program (TOP) is administered by the Bureau of the Fiscal Service. TOP collects delinquent debts owed to federal and state agencies by offsetting (reducing) federal payments.

## Types of Payments Subject to Offset

- Federal tax refunds
- Federal salary payments
- Social Security benefits (limited to 15% for most debts)
- Vendor and contractor payments
- Federal retirement benefits

## Exempt Payments

The following payments are generally NOT subject to offset:
- Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
- VA disability compensation
- Certain federal salary amounts below threshold

## Offset Priority Order

When multiple debts exist, offsets are applied in this order:
1. Child support
2. Federal tax debts
3. Other federal agency debts
4. State income tax debts
5. Unemployment compensation debts

## Notification Requirements

Before an offset occurs, you must receive:
- **Pre-offset notice**: At least 60 days before offset for most debts
- **Debt amount and creditor agency**
- **Right to inspect and copy records**
- **Right to dispute the debt**
- **Right to request hardship review**

## How to Dispute an Offset

1. **Contact the creditor agency** (not TOP or IRS) - the agency listed on your offset notice
2. **Submit dispute within 60 days** of the pre-offset notice
3. **Provide documentation** supporting your dispute
4. **Request review hearing** if available

## Hardship Exemption

You may request a hardship exemption if the offset would cause severe financial hardship. Requirements:
- Demonstrate inability to meet basic living expenses
- Provide financial documentation
- Submit request to creditor agency (not TOP)

## Offset Inquiry

To find out why your refund was offset:
- Call TOP at 1-800-304-3107
- Have your Social Security Number ready
- TOP will provide the creditor agency contact information

Reference: 31 CFR Part 285 - Debt Collection Authorities Under the Debt Collection Improvement Act of 1996
